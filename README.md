## What is an ITIN?

An **ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number)** is a tax ID number issued by the IRS for individuals who need to file US taxes but are not eligible for a Social Security Number (SSN). It is required for:

- Filing US federal tax returns (Form 1040-NR)
- Receiving US income (freelance, dividends, royalties, rental income)
- Opening a US bank account or investment account
- Applying for a US EIN (Employer Identification Number) as a foreign business owner
- US real estate investment and FIRPTA compliance
- Treaty benefits and withholding tax reduction
- US scholarship or fellowship income

---

## Who Needs an ITIN?

- **Non-resident aliens** with US tax obligations
- **Foreign nationals** receiving income from US sources
- **International investors** in US real estate or stocks
- **Foreign business owners** with US LLCs or corporations
- **Dependents or spouses** of US citizens/residents
- **Students** on F-1 / J-1 visas receiving stipends or fellowships
- **YouTube creators** and online sellers earning from US platforms (Google AdSense, Amazon, etc.)

## Common ITIN Use Cases

### 🏠 US Real Estate Investment
Foreign investors purchasing US property must comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act). An ITIN is required to file tax returns and claim withholding tax refunds.

### 💼 US LLC or Corporation Ownership
Non-US residents forming a US LLC often need an ITIN alongside an EIN to file taxes, open business bank accounts, and claim treaty benefits.

### 🎥 YouTube / Google AdSense
Google requires non-US creators to submit tax info (Form W-8BEN). With an ITIN, you can reduce withholding from 30% to your treaty rate (often 0–15%).

### 📈 US Stock / Dividend Income
Dividend income from US stocks is subject to 30% withholding. An ITIN allows you to file and claim a refund based on your country's tax treaty with the US.

### 🎓 Scholarship / Fellowship Income
Non-resident students receiving US fellowship stipends need an ITIN to file Form 1040-NR and potentially claim tax treaty exemptions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does it take to get an ITIN?**  
The IRS typically processes ITIN applications in 6–8 weeks after submission. Applications submitted during peak tax season (January–April) may take longer.

**Do I need to travel to the US?**  
No. Our entire service is 100% remote. You never need to visit a US consulate or embassy.

**Do I need to mail my passport?**  
No. As an IRS Certified Acceptance Agent (CAA), we can certify your identity remotely. Your original passport stays with you.

**Can I apply from outside the US?**  
Yes. We serve clients from every country. All communication and document submission is done online.

**What if my ITIN application is rejected?**  
We will resubmit your application at no additional cost if the rejection was due to an error on our part.

**Can I use an ITIN instead of an SSN?**  
An ITIN is for tax filing purposes only. It cannot be used for Social Security benefits, work authorization, or as a substitute for an SSN.

**Does an ITIN expire?**  
ITINs issued after 2012 expire if not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive years. We also offer ITIN renewal services.
